# A Review of Deep Learning Models for Age-Friendly Pedestrian Environment Assessment

**Student ID:** 22000077 | **Name:** Kim Minje

---

## Overview

This report reviews studies that apply deep learning to pedestrian environment assessment, with a focus on street view image-based approaches in urban research.

As cities are aging, safe and comfortable walking environments are becoming increasingly important for older adults - supporting their mobility, health, and access to daily services such as hospitals, parks, welfare facilities, and public transit.

---

## Scope of Review

### 1. CNN-Based Visual Feature Extraction
Review of CNN models used to extract visual features from street view images

### 2. Semantic Segmentation
Review of models that classify urban elements - sidewalks, roads, trees, buildings, sky, and vehicles - at the pixel level

### 3. Object Detection
Review of models that identify pedestrian facilities and obstacles in street environments

---

## Objectives

- Compare **data**, **models**, and **urban indicators** used in previous studies
- Discuss applicability to age-friendly urban planning and identify key limitations
